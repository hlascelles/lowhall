Low Hall is an old stone-built farmhouse in the Duddon Valley, one of the finest and most secluded parts of the Lake District. The house stands in farmland with impressive views of the fells in all directions.

Low Hall has been owned by only one family since it was restored in the 1960s, and is treated very much as a family home. The style is old-fashioned comfort with polished wood and deep armchairs, though with all modern amenities.

The house can accommodate up to ten people and is available year-round on personal recommendation for a consideration which covers the running costs.
