What to do

At the top of Scafell Pike, England's highest peak

The Duddon Valley is one of the least known parts of the Lake District: a traditional farming valley of green fields, dry stone walls and grazing sheep. There are few outsiders, but an abundance of natural features: high fells, rocky crags, waterfalls, the meandering Duddon , even a Roman fort.

Low Hall offers a wide range of activities from the doorstep: strolls along the Duddon and up to the pub in Seathwaite, low fell walking along pack horse trails, or high fell walking up nearby Caw Fell (nearly 2000 ft) and the Old Man of Coniston (2600 ft). The house is within an hour's drive of the big Lakeland peaks: Scafell, Helvellyn, Langdale Pikes etc.

The Duddon Valley also offers rock climbing, swimming, canoeing (graded 3 with some easy 4), fishing and bird-watching.

Low fell walking

Local outings include Muncaster (castle tour, rhododendrons, and an owl centre), a ride on the Ravenglass narrow gauge railway, "Ratty", Wordsworth's Dove Cottage at Grasmere, and Beatrix Potter's house at Sawrey. Sizergh Castle and Holker Hall are also not far away. John Ruskin's house at Brantwood can be visited by a ride on the steam launch Gondola across Lake Coniston.

Children's outings include The World of Beatrix Potter in Ambleside, the Aquarium of the Lakes at Newby Bridge, and Dalton Zoo.
